Brand & Design Guidelines

The thinking behind the refresh

A short, plain-English guide to the decisions behind the Skotte Group website refresh — what changed, what we deliberately kept, and why. Written to sit alongside the site and to brief production.

Skotte Group•Website refresh•v1 · for review
01

At a glance

A refresh, not a rebuild. We kept the structure and story you know, and sharpened the brand, the imagery and the words so the site reads like the class-leading advisory firm you are.

Kept the logo, exactlyYour official logo is used unaltered everywhere. Header and footer sit on light backgrounds so it always reads cleanly.
Colour with heritageThe palette draws on the blue and red of our Scottish and Danish roots — the origin of “Skotte” — so brand and story line up.
Brighter, purposeful photographyIconic Scottish and Danish landscapes, chosen bright and legible — the moody, grey shots are gone.
Your words, verbatimValues, services and team bios use the latest wording from your documents. Nothing invented.
02

Logo

The logo is a fixed asset. We use your official artwork, unaltered — no recolours, no redraws, no effects — across the navigation, footer, mobile menu and favicon.

How we protect it

  • Because it is a coloured mark on a transparent background, the header and footer are kept light (white / warm paper) so the logo always sits on the background it was designed for.
  • It is decoupled from the hero photography — the logo never overlaps a busy image, so it stays crisp and never competes for attention.
  • Clear space and minimum size are respected in every placement; the pennant and wordmark are never separated.
03

04

Typography

Type does a lot of the heavy lifting for a boutique advisory brand. We pair a refined display serif with a clean, highly legible sans — considered, trustworthy, and unmistakably not a template.

05

Photography

The landing photo on each page reflects the Scottish/Danish heritage behind the name. We tightened the selection to bright, purposeful landscapes — and set two firm rules so images always support the message rather than fight it.

Rules

  • Bright, not gloomy. Sunlit, blue-sky or high-key scenes only. The grey, overcast mountain shots have been replaced.
  • Text stays legible. Every hero uses a graduated dark scrim behind the headline, so white type reads clearly on any image, at any screen size.

06

Voice & content

All copy comes straight from your documents — this is a design exercise, not a rewrite.

  • Our values use the five current values Commitment · Honesty · Growth · Kindness · Teamwork with your exact definitions.
  • What we do presents the five services in your preferred order and new wording, each linking through to the full page.
  • The Skotte difference is the three differentiators from the brief — Experience, Hard work, Commitment to excellence.
  • Team bios use the latest wording from the Team Profiles document, grouped by level, with Seaton shown as a Partner.
  • “What’s in a name?” carries the derivation of Skotte and the “we pronounce it ‘Scott’” line, as the brief requested.
  • Experience lists every project from your current site, mapped to the new service lines, with filters by service and by client plus pagination.
07

Layout, motion & “Make things happen”

  • Generous whitespace and a calm rhythm — the site breathes, which reads as premium and considered.
  • One component set — cards, buttons, chips, filters and the bio drawer are shared across every page for consistency.
  • Restrained motion — gentle scroll reveals and hover states; fully disabled for visitors who prefer reduced motion, and everything works without JavaScript.
  • “Make things happen” is carried through every page — in the closing band and in the footer tagline — as the brief asked.
  • Footer — the old “we are an expert…” paragraph is dropped in favour of the tagline, per the brief.
08

Decisions & answers

Answers to the questions raised on the first version — kept here as a record.

Where does the Scottish landscape come from — is this a big redesign for Maya?
Look & feel

It’s heritage, not a new theme. Your current site already leans on Scottish landmarks; we simply chose brighter, more purposeful landscapes. The page structure mirrors the existing site, so for production this is a design direction, not a re-architecture.

Is the logo correct now?
Brand

Yes — your official logo, used unaltered, on light chrome throughout.

How is the Scottish/Danish heritage shown now?
Brand

Subtly — through the colour palette and the “What’s in a name?” story, not literal flags. At the client’s request, all flag graphics have been removed from the site.

Has the font changed?
Typography

Deliberately — Fraunces for headlines and Inter for text, for an editorial, boutique-advisory feel that reads as considered rather than generic.

Is all the new content included?
Content

Yes — values, services and team bios all use the new wording from your documents, and Experience covers every project from your site.

Do the “what we do” links work?
Function

Yes — every service links through to the full What we do page, and the whole site shares one working navigation.

The “Why Skotte” heritage story — was that from the brief?
Brief

Yes — the brief asked for a “What’s in a name?” section explaining the Scottish/Danish roots. We keep it as a light touch through colour and story, without leaning on flags.

Can this roll forward to the other pages?
Scope

Done — Team, What we do, Experience, Careers and Contact all share one design system, ready for review and production.
